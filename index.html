American painter and a major figure in the Abstract Expressionist Movement

Here's a timeline of Jackson Pollock's life & art practice.

  • 1912 - Born in Cody, Wyoming
  • 1928 - Enrolled at Manual Arts High School, from which he was expelled
  • 1936 - Was introduced to the use of liquid paint at an experimental workshop in New York City by the Mexican muralist David Alfaro Siqueiros. He later used paint pouring as one of several techniques on canvases of the early 1940s, such as Male and Female and Composition with Pouring I
  • 1947 - 1950 - Pollock's most famous paintings were made during the "drip period". He became famous following an August 8, 1949, four-page spread in Life magazine that asked, "Is he the greatest living painter in the United States?"
  • 1951 - Pollock's work after 1951 was darker in color, including a collection painted in black on unprimed canvases. These paintings have been referred to as his Black pourings and when he exhibited them at the Betty Parsons Gallery in New York, none of them sold.
  • 1955 - Happenings impresario Allan Kaprow wrote in Art News in honor of the late artist: “[Pollock] left us at the point where we must become preoccupied with and even dazzled by the space and objects of our everyday life…these, I am sure, will be the alchemies of the 1960s.
  • 1956 - On August 11, at 10:15 p.m., Pollock died in a single-car crash in his Oldsmobile convertible while driving under the influence of alcohol.Four months after his death, Pollock was given a memorial retrospective exhibition at the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York City.
  • 2000 - Release of biographical film Pollock, based on the Pulitzer Prize-winning biography, Jackson Pollock: An American Saga, directed by and starring Ed Harris
  • 2004 - One: Number 31, 1950 was ranked the eighth-most influential piece of modern art in a poll of 500 artists, curators, critics, and dealers.
  • 2006 - Pollock's No. 5, 1948 became the world's most expensive painting, when it was sold privately to an undisclosed buyer for the sum of US$140 million.
